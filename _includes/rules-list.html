General

GDG DevFest Omsk consists of one-day hackathon (December 14), which is coming after a short educational seminar (December 13).

Themes of GDG DevFest 2014:

  1. Wearable Devices
  2. Modern Web-Frontend
  3. Augmented & Virtual Reality

It is required that you use at least one of theme technologies in the list below:

  • Polymer, Web Componets, AngularJS, Dart, БЭМ
  • Android Wear SDK, Pebble SDK, Apple iWatch SDK
  • Glass Mirror API, Glass Development Kit
  • Cardboard VR SDK, Oculus SDK

To take part you should perform the following steps:

  1. Come up with original idea
  2. Gather your team
  3. Apply for the event
  4. Get approval from the organizers

Idea

The secret of successful idea is that it's simple and interesting for you to make. We do not encourage you to impress others with complexity, yet well-built realization is the key to win.

Team

Required team size is 2-3 participants. Team members and idea must be determined by the time of application! Well-built team and role playing are vital for successful programming on hackathons, so you are strongly recommended to discuss your idea and roles before the event.

Application

Use TimePad service to apply for the DevFest. It is important to select number of tickets equal to number of members of your team. Be careful while filling in the application form. Once you finish, organizers will consider your team to be event participants.

Approval

In case your idea corresponds to the regulations above, sounds realistic and is practically perspective, you are likely to get approved in a couple days from application day. Approval is sent in a form of a special letter, containing tickets. Only ticket-holders can take part in the event.

In the other case you will get a letter of disapproval. If your application was disapproved, feel free to apply again with a different idea.

Seminar

Every ticket holder is suggested to pay a visit on the educational and discussional seminar, which takes place on a precending day, December, 13th. During the seminar we will go through some useful hackathon theory and talk on any topic chosen by participants.
